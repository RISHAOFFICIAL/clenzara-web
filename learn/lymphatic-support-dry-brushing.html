Lymphatic Support: Moving Your Body's Sewage System

Published May 28, 2026 • 3 min read
Medical Disclaimer: Educational content only — not medical advice. Always consult a healthcare provider before starting any supplement or cleanse protocol. Learn more

The lymphatic system is often called the 'sewage system' of the body, but unlike the circulatory system, it has no pump. It relies entirely on movement, pressure changes, and gravity to circulate lymph fluid and eliminate waste.

Dry brushing is a powerful mechanical stimulus that encourages the flow of lymph toward the heart, where it can be filtered and excreted. Incorporating a 5-minute dry brushing routine before your morning shower can dramatically improve skin emunctory function. Pair this with 'rebounding' (gentle jumping on a mini-trampoline) or even just brisk walking to ensure that as you begin your cleanse, your internal waste-disposal pathways are active and unobstructed.
